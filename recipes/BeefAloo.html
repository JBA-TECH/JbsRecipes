Simple Garlic Chicken Curry

================================

Ingredients

  • Beef: 1lb (500g) braising beef, cubed.
  • Potatoes (Aloo): 2 large potatoes, peeled and cubed.
  • Aromatics: 1 large onion (sliced), 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste.
  • Base: 2 tomatoes (chopped) or ½ cup tomato puree.
  • Spices - 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • Spices - 1 tbsp Kashmiri chili powder (for color without too much heat)
  • Spices - 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • Spices - 1 tsp garam masala
  • Whole spices: 1 cinnamon stick, 3 cardamom pods, 1 bay leaf.

Instructions

  1. Sear the Beef: Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pot or pressure cooker. Brown the beef cubes in batches to lock in flavor, then remove and set aside.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: In the same oil, add the whole spices. Once fragrant, add the onions and fry until golden brown. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste for 1 minute.
  3. The Masala Base: Add the chopped tomatoes and the powdered spices (except garam masala). Cook until the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  4. Slow Simmer: Return the beef to the pot. Add enough water to cover the meat, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours (or 20 mins in a pressure cooker) until the beef is nearly tender.
  5. Add the Aloo: Toss in your potato cubes. Continue cooking for another 15–20 minutes until both the beef and potatoes are fork-tender.
  6. Finish: Stir in the garam masala and a handful of fresh cilantro (coriander).
Summary of Content Ingredients: Contains beef, potatoes, aromatics, tomato base, and specific spices. Method: Includes steps for searing, sautéing, creating the masala base, simmering the beef for 1–1.5 hours, cooking the potatoes, and finishing the dish.

Tips for Success

  • The Potato Trick: Fry the potato cubes separately in a little oil before adding them to the curry. This helps them hold their shape and adds a better texture.
  • Consistency: If the gravy is too thin, mash one or two potato cubes into the sauce to thicken it naturally.

Sides???

    To go with your beef and potato curry, you might consider these classic side dishes:

    • Garlic Naan: The soft, charred bread is perfect for scooping up the rich masala base and tender beef.
    • Cumin Rice (Jeera Rice): A fragrant, fluffy rice that complements the warmth of the cinnamon and cardamom used in the curry.
    • Kachumber Salad: A fresh mix of chopped cucumbers, onions, and tomatoes to provide a crisp contrast to the slow-simmered beef.
    • Raita: A cooling yogurt-based side that balances the heat from the Kashmiri chili powder.
    • Papadums: Crispy, thin Indian flatbreads that add a nice crunch and complement the rich curry.
