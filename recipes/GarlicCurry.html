Simple Garlic Chicken Curry

================================

Ingredients

  • Chicken: 500g boneless thighs (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • Garlic: 8–10 cloves (finely minced or crushed)
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece (finely grated or pasted)
  • Onion: 1 large (finely diced)
  • Tomatoes: 400g crushed tomatoes or tomato purée
  • Fat: 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Spices: 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tbsp coriander, 2 tsp chili powder, and 1 tsp garam masala
  • Liquid: 100–200ml chicken stock or water
  • Salt: To taste
  • Finishers: 1 tsp dried fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi), fresh cilantro, and 1–2 green chilies (optional)
  • Creamy Touch: A splash of coconut milk or heavy cream (optional)

Instructions

  1. Fry Garlic & Onions: Heat oil/ghee in a large pan over medium heat. Add the garlic and fry until light golden brown. (Optional: Remove half the garlic and set aside for garnish). Add diced onions and fry until golden brown (6–8 minutes).
  2. Bloom the Aromatics: Add the ginger and any remaining garlic; sauté for 1 minute. Stir in the turmeric, cumin, coriander, and chili powder. Cook for 30–60 seconds, adding a splash of water if the spices start to stick.
  3. Build the Base: Stir in the tomatoes. Cook for about 5 minutes until they break down and the oil begins to separate from the sauce.
  4. Brown the Chicken: Add the chicken pieces. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring frequently, until the chicken is browned on all sides and coated in the spice base.
  5. Simmer: Pour in the stock or water. Bring to a gentle simmer, cover, and cook for 15–20 minutes until the chicken is tender.
  6. The Finish: Stir in the garam masala, fenugreek leaves, and optional cream. Simmer uncovered for 2 minutes to thicken. Season with salt to taste.
  7. Optional:Squeeze of lemon

Tips for Success

  • Garlic Texture: Use a mix of sliced garlic for texture and crushed garlic to melt into the sauce.
  • Consistency: If the sauce is too thin, simmer uncovered for the last 5 minutes.
  • Serving: Best enjoyed with fluffy basmati rice or buttery naan bread.
  • Hotness: Remove the green chilli seeds or replace with green peppers reduce the chilli amount
